Science, Reasoning, and Critical Thinking, Crispain Jago


I chose this visualization because of the spin it put on Harry Beck's original London Subway map. Instead of subway stops being presented in a linear and temporal fashion, Jango presents branches of science. By adding a graphic legend in the background corresponding to century, the legend becomes part of the visualization instead of a separate piece of auxiliary information. While the visualization does seem a bit cluttered, the idea of presenting the information in this fashion and not the aesthetic value, was what appealed to me.

I think the author does a good job of using lines of different color and type as a compact way to display data and using symmetry among the lines to add aesthetic value and conform to Tufte's Principles of Graphic Display.
