How Quantitative Easing Spiralled, Mark McCormick


Much like the first example of an Insightful Information Visualization that I referenced in example #1, this visualization presents a traditionally non-spatial dataset (in this case currency) as an area instead of a number. I already mentioned how this adds insight when comparing among the events, but this visualization adds a temporal aspect also. Instead of the traditional linear temporal events as a vertical or horizontal line, the author uses the "spiral" reference from the title. I think this is a very clever use of a metaphor in the title, the graphical display, and the negative connotations that are normally attached to something "spiraling out of control".

Of the Principles of Graphic Display outlined by Tufte, I think the author makes great use of symmetry and conveying a sense of balance, proportion, and relative scale within this visualization.
