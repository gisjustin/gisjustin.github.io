Billion Euro-o-gram, David McCandless


I really like this type of visualization because of the way it graphically presents a traditionally non-spatial (in this case currency) dataset into area. When presenting currency amounts in the billions, and comparing among various sizes of amounts, presenting the data as an area rather than just a number, seems to emphasize the comparisons and not the number. In this specific example, while the 44 Billion Euros spent on the Iraq war in 2003 is a large number, when graphically comparing it to the 1047 Billion Euro war on terror, it adds an overall context and puts the values into perspective.

I believe this visualization is visually appealing and deos a very good job of conveying a sense of balance, proportion, and relative scale as outlined in Edward Tufte's Principles of Graphic Display.
